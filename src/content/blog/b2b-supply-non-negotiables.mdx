## What Procurement Teams Actually Need From a Granule Manufacturer

When a procurement head at a mid-to-large fertilizer brand evaluates a new manufacturing partner, they are not just evaluating price per metric tonne. They are evaluating **supply certainty** — the confidence that when they place an order, the right product will arrive on time, at specification, every time.

Four variables determine whether a manufacturer can deliver that certainty.

## 1. Capacity That Matches Your Demand Curve

Capacity is not just about peak volume. It is about whether your supplier can absorb your demand spikes without pushing your order back three weeks.

A manufacturer with a 50 MT/day ceiling might look fine on a quarterly basis, but during seasonal demand peaks — pre-Kharif or pre-Rabi — that same manufacturer may be juggling ten clients and your order gets deprioritised.

**What to ask:** What is the dedicated daily capacity available for my SKU? Can you demonstrate 24/7 operational records?

## 2. Testing Discipline Across Every Stage

A manufacturer who only tests finished goods discovers problems too late.

The right testing regime covers:

- **Raw material inspection** on every incoming batch
- **In-Process Quality Control (IPQC)** during granulation and coating
- **Finished goods testing** before dispatch

FCO approval is the baseline — it means the system is audited, not just claimed.

**What to ask:** Can I see your lab test reports for the last three batches of my product type? What is your rejection rate at IPQC?

## 3. Dispatch Capability That Protects Your Timeline

Production capacity and dispatch capability are not the same thing. A manufacturer can have excellent production numbers and still create bottlenecks at the loading dock.

Dispatch capability includes:

- Dedicated truck bays for timely loading
- Finished goods storage that is organised, labelled, and FIFO-managed
- Advance notice of dispatch with proper documentation (batch report, invoice, weight certificate)

**What to ask:** What is your average lead time from order confirmation to dispatch?

## 4. Formulation Flexibility When Your Brief Changes

The fertilizer market moves. A regulatory update, a new active ingredient, or a customer request can change your formulation brief on short notice.

A manufacturing partner who can only produce one granule type is a constraint on your ability to respond. Formulation flexibility means:

- Ability to change base materials (e.g., Gypsum-dominant to Dolomite-dominant) without a 6-week lead time
- Custom coating recipes developed and validated within your product development cycle
- Trial batch capability before committing to full production

## The Single Most Useful Question to Ask

If you could only ask one question of a prospective manufacturing partner:

> *"Can you share references from two clients who have been with you for over two years?"*

Longevity of the client relationship is the clearest proxy for all four non-negotiables above.
